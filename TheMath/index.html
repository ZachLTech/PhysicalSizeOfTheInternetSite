The Math That Calculates Digital Internet Size to a Physical Datacenter Size

Algorithm Components

Digital Size Calculation

First things first is getting an up to date digital size of the internet (i.e. in terms of TB or GB for example). The first steps to doing this just involve a few google searches to find some sort of reference point as to how large the internet is or was at a certain point in time and also find out how fast it grows. I found that at the beginning of 2023, as far as we know, the internet was around 106 trillion terabytes large and now grows at a pace of around 45 terabytes per second. With this information, I simply get the date and time whenever my stat pages are open and multiply the amount of seconds it has been since the beginning of 2023 by 45 and add that amount to 106 trillion (the internet size at the beginning of 2023) in order to get a real time internet size.

Calculating the # of Drives Needed

Knowing the internet size is around 106 Trillion terabytes, to find the amount of storage devices needed I just divide this 106 trillion by the amount of terabytes stored in each storage device. Since this system is just simple division run by my coded program, I was able to make the amount of terabytes stored in each storage device a mutable variable (meaning it can be changed at any time). Having this as a mutable variable is how I was able to make the site allow the user to change the amount of storage that each device can hold which affects the amount of storage devices needed. This causes a domino effect resulting in the length, width, and price statistics to change which I thought would be an interesting feature.
